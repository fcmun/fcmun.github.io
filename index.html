About FCMUN

Five College Model United Nations offers delegates small, interactive, dynamic and exhilarating committees run by the best students of the Five College Consortium, including Mount Holyoke College, University of Massachusetts at Amherst, Smith College, Hampshire College and Amherst College. We believe in offering the most stimulating crises and facilitating provocative debate in every committee, and pride ourselves on offering one of the lowest delegate to staff ratios on the circuit. Five College Model United Nations has established a tradition of engaging and unique committees and a high-level of debate.

Erdoğan Cabinet: cabinet of the Turkish President

Crisis Director: Nooria Puri
puri23n@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

This committee is the cabinet of Turkish President, Recep Tayyip Erdoğan (recently elected) and his cabinet members. The cabinet will have to deal with the current ISIS crisis that is affecting Turkey, Turkey's entry into the UNSC, the Kurdish uprisings, women's rights violations as well as other social and economic tensions that the citizens of the country are currently facing. This is a contemporary politics committee, which may borrow some influences from Turkish history. Erdogan will be the chair.

The Red Cavalry: the Russian Civil War

Crisis Director: Nicole Daphnis
daphn22n@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

This committee will play out events during the Russian Civil War of 1917-1922, from the perspective of the Bolshevik Party. The two main tasks of the committee would be to carry out a successful war effort against the White Army while maintaining control of the country. The committee members also need to figure out how to actually run the newly formed Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic. How will they enact the ideas of Marxism-Leninism, especially when there is a struggle for influence between Trotsky and Stalin? What about the general party politics to earn Lenin's favor as his health is declining?

2050: Tech-Utopia Summit

Crisis Director: Onji Bae
bae22o@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

It is the year 2050 and the United States are in the forefront of leading the world into the next age of peace thanks to the tech powers of Google, Tesla, Apple, and more. Renewable energy is well on its way replacing gas and oil. Bitcoin has been recognized as an official currency in many parts of the world. Drones have efficiently decreased warfare and saved the country billions of dollars. However, the weakened powers of oil companies, Wall Street, and conservative politicians are ready to give their last shot in maintaining their status in the U.S. government. Will the tech industry companies come together to defeat the "old powers" and re-define Capitol Hill? Or will they turn their backs against each other and seek autonomy?

Lord of the Rings

Crisis Director: Daniela Rakhlina-Powsner
rakhl22d@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

It is the year 3018 of the third age, and the Council of Elrond has closed. Evidence of the rise of the Dark Lord Sauron’s return has been brought to the key players of Middle Earth so a meeting has been called for them to decided how to best combat the threat. The “One Ring” is currently in the possession of a young hobbit named Frodo and in order to defeat Sauron it must be destroyed in the fires of Mount Doom. Sources of conflict will be related to the individual characters’ actions as well as the characters of Golem and various followers of Sauron attempting to steal the ring.The deliberations will continue until Sauron comes to power, or the ring is finally destroyed. Perhaps even some of you will fall prey to its allure. Will you stay true to the light or will Sauron manage to recruit you to his side? Will you band together to defeat Sauron, or will the Dark Lord rise to power once again?

The Cuban Revolution

Crisis Director: Kaila Cantens
cante22k@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

Fulgencio Batista has been the Dictator of Cuba since 1952 and a major supporter of the United States. Little did the regime know, Fidel Castro has united with his brother Raul and Ernesto Che Guevara to create the 26th of July revolutionary movement to overthrow the regime. The committee will start in July 7th, 1955 and will continue to 1959 when the revolution lead by Fidel Castro overcame the Flugencio Batista regime. However, the committees decisions will show whether they will succeed at creating the revolution or not. The committee will end with the death of Che Guevara in 1967. Whether he dies will also depend on the committee and their actions.

Reconstruction Era United States

Crisis Director: Elisabeth Lee
lee35e@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

With the end of the Civil War and the surrender of General Lee at Appamattox Court House, the major players of the age are faced with pressing issues such as the re-integration of the seceded states, rights for former slaves, and negotiations with the Confederate leaders. As members of Lincoln’s cabinet, Congressional leaders of the Radical Republicans, Moderate Republicans and Democrats and leaders of the North and South converge to decide what route the United States should take in bringing this nation together, the success and failure of Reconstruction is in their hands. Will they go in the same direction as before or change the course of American history?

Democracy and Empire: Taisho Period Japan

Crisis Director: Kelly Charest
chare23k@mtholyoke.edu
Background Guide

At the end of WWI, the Imperial diet of Taisho Japan is faced with many issues . Japan was reaping the economic benefits of the wartime economy and was engaged in the simultaneous pursuits of both civil rights and empire. The Diet was caught between the emperor and the military which acted independently of civilian government, facing a post-war crash including labor strikes, protests by rural tenant farmers, and further economic recession caused by serious earthquake. The period ended with the staged bombing of the Manchurian incident and diet ended in Japan. Will the parliamentary government be preserved or will history repeat itself in this committee?

Taiping Rebellion JCC

Crisis Director: Julian del Prado
Background Guide

Qing Dynasty Advisers China's Qing Dynasty faced an uprising of unprecedented size and brutality during the Taiping Rebellion, and was forced to make difficult choices in regards to tradition and governance. Seemingly overnight, the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom under Hong Xiuquan had seized a vast portion of Southern China. Advisers to the Xianfeng Emperor were faced with running a nation reeling from the Opium War, where widespread famine and corruption weakened the central government even more than abusive foreign treaties. Faced with holding together an empire, it will be the task of the Emperor and his advisers to adapt to changing times by confronting and addressing the many weaknesses of the empire. Failure to maintain legitimacy in the eyes of foreign powers may lead them to recognizing the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom as a legitimate empire; however, conceding too much to foreign powers could compromise the name and legacy of China forever.

Emergency UN Summit on the Political Crisis in Thailand

Crisis Director:

It is May 22nd, 2014, and Thailand is in a state of political uncertainty. Thailand has faced political tension between the Thai Rak Thai party, led mostly by the Shinawatra family, and the People's Democrat Party, for the past decade. Now, Yingluck Shinawatra has been let down from her position as prime minister, and the main opponent party, the People's Democratic Reform Committee, has prevented votes from taking place. The military, which has executed 11 successful coup d’etats in the last 22 years, is becoming increasingly involved in the current conflict. This cabinet, made up of the members of the UN Security Council and the main Thai members of the conflict, will examine the immediate actions needed to secure short and long-term political peace.

Susan Bissell

Chief of Child Protection, Programme Division UNICEF

A native of Canada, Susan first served UNICEF in 1987, in New York, in what was then called the Division of Information and Public Affairs. Thereafter she returned to the University of Toronto to complete a Master’s degree in law, economics and international relations. Susan then resumed her work at UNICEF, in the Sri Lanka country office, and focused on children in especially difficult circumstances (CEDC). From there Susan moved to Bangladesh and maintained her CEDC concentration, positioning UNICEF particularly on child labour at a time when it was attracting considerable international attention.
In 1997, Susan again commenced academic work, in a doctoral degree in public health and medical anthropology at the WHO Key Center for Women’s Health, Faculty of Medicine, University of Melbourne. While completing her doctorate, Susan also worked with Trudie Styler and the Bangladeshi film team Catherine and Tareque Masud to produce the documentary "A Kind of Childhood." The film screened widely at film festivals in North America, Europe, and Asia, and appeared on Canadian, American, and British television. In 2005, it had a second screening at the London Human Rights Watch Film Festival.
Susan came back to UNICEF in 2001 as the Chief of Child Protection in India. In 2004, she transferred to the Innocenti Research Center, where she led a research unit and a number of studies. These included a 62-country study on the implementation of the general measures of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child, and global research on the Palermo Protocol and child trafficking. Susan was also a member of the Editorial Board of the report of the UN Secretary General’s Study on Violence Against Children, which was released in 2006.
In 2009, Susan was appointed to her current position in New York, heading all of UNICEF’s Child Protection work. She oversees a team of professionals guiding efforts for children affected by armed conflict, child protection systems strengthening to prevent and respond to all forms of violence against children, and a range of other matters. UNICEF is active in child protection in 170 countries, and the New York team offers leadership, strategic vision, and technical support.
Susan was recently awarded an honourary Professorship at Barnard College/Columbia University. She also received the Dr. Jean Mayar Global Citizenship Award from Tufts University in 2012 and the Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee Medal in 2012. Susan was honoured to accept these on behalf of her UNICEF Child Protection colleagues around the world.
